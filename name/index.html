my name

My family name is 張 Zhang . It means something like ‘bowyer’ or ‘archer’. The pronunciation that I prefer in English is [dʒɑŋ]: [dʒ] as in John, [ɑ] as in car, and [ŋ] as in song.

My legal given name is Katherine. Most people should use this name to refer to me.

My Chinese given name is 采 Cai 真 zhen . It comes from the following passage in the writings of Zhuangzi.

古之至人，假道於仁，託宿於義，以遊逍遙之虛，食於苟簡之田，立於不貸之圃。逍遙，无為也；苟簡，易養也；不貸，无出也。古者謂是采真之遊。

— ICS Zhuangzi1 14/39/27-28</div>

Here are two translations of the last sentence:[^2]

… The ancients called this the outward visible sign of an inward and spiritual grace.
… The ancients called this the Enjoyment that Collects the True.

采 means ‘collect’, and 真 means ‘true’. Together, in the context of this passage, 采真 refers to true human nature.

A Concordance to the Zhuangzi (莊子逐字索引), ed. D.C. Lau, Ho Che Wah and Chen Fong Ching. ICS series (Hong Kong: Commercial Press, 2000). [^2]: Chuang Tzŭ: Mystic, Moralist, and Social Reformer, trans.
